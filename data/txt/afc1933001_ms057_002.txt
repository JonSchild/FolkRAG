General Delivery
Houston, Tex. 4/8/1939.

Dear Mr. Spivocke: At last the
machine and the records are all
in one place, put together and
seemingly in fine condition. When
the machine got to Port Aransas
it wouldn't budge. I drove it
to Austin where I know I could
get a competent mechanic. He 
found the trouble, and it was
real and not imaginary
trouble. The records met me
here. Tomorrow I make my 
first recording.

Alan advises me strongly
to use emerald-pointed cutting
needles, but Presto sent only
