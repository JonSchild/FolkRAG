FOLKLORE STUDY
 SCHEDULED AT I.U.
    ____________
Visiting Specialists Are
    On Faculty of 
Summer Institute.
       ______________
  Bloomington, Ind.,  June 27.----
(Special)---Indiana University's first
summer institute of folklore will
begin Monday,  with such visiting
folklore specialists as Alan Lomax,
assistant in charge of the archives
of American folk song of the
Library of Congress, and John
Jacob Niles, composer and 
folksong collector of Lexington, Ky.,
on the faculty.
   Dr. Stith Thompson of the I.U.
faculty, who is a former president
of the American Folklore Society
and an international authority on
the subject, and Herbert Halpert,
president of the Hoosier Folklore 
Society, also will be on the faculty.
Prof. Thompson is director of the
institute.
    The wide variety of activities in
the institute will be directed to-
ward the interests and needs of 
regular university students, 
practical folklore collectors, the
general public and the folklore
specialists themselves.

Emphasis On Tradition.
  Principal emphasis of the
institute will be placed on the
manifestation of tradition in tales, songs
and folk speech.  Fields to be
included in the institute are 
introduction to folklore, folk song and
folk music, Pan-American folklore
biography,  folk  tale  and  allied
forms,  folklore collecting and re-
cording, American dialects, field re-
cording in foreign texts and 
American Indian folklore.
   Others who will assist with the
conducting of this institute are 
Prof. Ralph S. Boggs, University of
North Carolina; Prof. George
Herzog,  Columbia  University;  Prof.
Charles F. Voegelin and Prof. 
Harold Whitehall, Indiana  University;
Prof. Harold W. Thompson, Cornell
University, president of the 
American Folklore Society, and Erminie
W. Voegelin, editor of the Journal
of American Folklore.
