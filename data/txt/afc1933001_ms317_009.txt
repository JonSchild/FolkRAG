     When their six-weeks course is complete, the trainees will
serve on field assignments with State and local health departments
throughout the nation. They will be paid by the Federal health
service to aid local health personnel hard pressed by problems created
by industrial and military establishments.
     The course will be opened at the Institute with addresses
on "General and Public Health Aspects of National Defense" by
Charles P. Taft, Assistant Coordinator of Health, Welfare and Related
Activities, Federal Security Agency and Doctor Parran. the addresses
will be given at 10 a.m. April 8, at the National Institute of Health,
Bethesda, Md.
     Successive six-weeks courses will be given at the Institute, 
until the personnel provided for under the emergency health and 
sanitation program have been recruited and trained.
