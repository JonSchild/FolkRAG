I  Analysis Program - Reporting

The director of the analysis program, the Research Director,
should be an individual trained and experienced in social science 
research.  The combination of psychology and sociology in the background
of this person would be best--better than a specialist in only 
one field or the other.  This combination is desirable because it is
sociological analysis that provides fullest understanding of the conditions
under which rumors flourish, and it is psychological analysis
which provides the fullest understanding of the implications of such
rumors as do appear.  The research director should be responsible for
the selection and training of field reporters and analysts.  He should
also maintain constant supervision over their work.
Reporters should be individually selected.  It would not be
wise to issue a general call for volunteers even with the intention
of selecting among the respondents.  Too much antagonism would be
aroused among those rejected.  In the case of some projects it may be
desirable to request public cooperation in sending in rumors.  Where such
requests are made it should be recognized by the project directors that
this is done, not so much to provide the project with rumors for analysis,
as to relieve those members of the community who may feel that they
wish to refer some particular rumor to the proper agency.  With this in
mind, the procedure to be recommended for referral of rumors is the mail.
