NEGRO GANG SONGS
1.     Take This Hammer.  A.A.F.S.#     .  Willie Williams 
and gang, Richmond, Va., 1936.  Stanzas from Georgia, 
Alabama, North Carolina, and Florida.  See, O & J, 
N.W.S. p, 105, p. 112, p. 120; also Po' Laz'us, Ev'y 
Mail Day, Lomas, ABFS, White, p. 259.
2.     Don't Talk About It.  A.A.F.S.#268.  Four Negro men, 
State Penitentiary, Raleigh, N.C.  Final two stanzas 
from Texas 1935.  See, O&J, NWS, p. 76, p. 106.
3.     Didn' Ol' John Cross the Water.  A.A.F.S.#265B1.  Group of Negro men, 
Reed Camp, S.C.  1934. See, O&J, NWS, p. 193;  
McIlhenny, p. 154; Pauline Johnson, V.2, p. 63;
4.     Lord, It's Almost Done.  A.A.F.S.#225A4.  Negro man, 
Wetumka, Ala. Oct. 1934.  See, O&J, NWS, p. 258; BB, p. 26.
5.     Ain't Workin' Song.  A.A.F.S.#1336B2. Charley Campbell, 
Alabama State Docks, Mobile, Ala., July 1937.
6.     I Got to Roll.  A.A.F.S.#     . From the singing of 
Bess Brown Lomax and Alan Lomax,  learned from "Black 
Sampson", who sang it them in the Nashville  Penitentiary, 
1937.  See, O&J, p. 101.
7.     You Kicked and Stomped and Beat Me.  A.A.F.S.#160A3.
Bowlegs, State Penitentiary, Parchman, Mississippi, 
1933.
