Dear Sir:

Under date of August 1, 1942 Mr. Heber R. Harper, Regional Director, Social Security Board, Region XI, Denver, Colorado instructed me to make a study of rumors which came to my attention during the period August 3 until noon August 8.

In accordance with this instruction, I am pleased to report as follows: During the period August 3 to 8, inclusive, in connection with my  regular duties, I rode the busses on 13 occasions, visited one barber shop, two hotels, two cigar stands, three department stores, four different lunch rooms, talked with fourteen business men, listened to numerous conversations between pedestrains, stood in front of the newspaper office on seven occasions, and listened to the conversations of many individuals who visited my office for the purpose of securing Social Security account numbers.

All of the following events occured in the City of Denver on the dates shown.
Very truly yours,
[signature]
J. S. Fehr, Acting Manager
Denver Field Office
