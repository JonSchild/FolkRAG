Near our small ranch north of Meridian, Texas, when I was a child a little creek ran, discharging, a mile or so away, a clear swift running stream of water into the Bosque River. This creek make a wide sweeping curve just east of our ranch [?] in almost circular form on the inner side of the circle several hundreds of acres of flat, grass -covered meadow land-- and ideal spot for bedding down the large herds of long horn cattle which almost daily in the Spring of the year traveled past our ranch home headed for Doge City, Kansas. Here ran a branch of the Old Chisholm Trail , famous in song, story and legend in the Middle West [Southwest]. 
The protected glade as a bedding place for the cattle and a nearby spring for cold water made with spot the favorite camping place for trail herds. When the cattle were settled for the night. two cow-boys were always left as sentinels to guard the sleeping herd. Starting at one point of the [camp] circle and riding in opposite directions each cowboy would follow the rim of the herd until he reached the bank of the creek, wheel his horse, double back [on the flank until] to meet his companion, turn and repeat until relieved by the next watch. A watch was usually three or four hours. [and] As these young men rode about the cattle under the [?] stars in the darkness of the night they often sang songs.
