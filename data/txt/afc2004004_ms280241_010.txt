4) It fell upon a day and a bonny summer's day, 
To face the lassie's father, some money he had to pay, 
To face the lassie's father some money he had to pay, 
To the Laird of the Dentidoonbye.

5) "O Good morning, how do you do? 
How is your daughter Jannety noo?
How is your daughter Janety noo
Since I laid her in the Dentidoonbye?" 

6) "O my wee Janet she is no very well, 
My wee Janet she looks uncou' pale, 
My wee Janet she kowks at her kail
Since ye laid her in the Dentidoonbye."

7) For he took her by the lilywhite hand, 
He showed her round his rooms, they were twenty-one, 
He placed the keys intae her hand 
Saying, "You're lady of the Dentidoonbye."

8) "O," says the old man, "What will I do?"
"O," says the old wife, "I'll dance till I die."
"O," says the old man, "I think I'll do that tae, 
Since  she's made Lady of the Dentidoonbye."
