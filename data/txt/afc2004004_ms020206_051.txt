R: Nice timber on it, and then it was real good land. I know when
my father made his house on it, we had the cotton on his farm there,
you know, and one year, and none of the farmers around there had to
been able to do that. And it was real good land; we had some bottom
land there that would make - oh - some bald and a half, couldn't bring
anything extra for it. I've know cotton there, you couldn't pick
a row; you just had to get in there in the middle and just pick.
Yes, sir, somebody in the next middle, you know, and it went on that
way. We had some good land, fine land.
So they taken all of the timber off it, and all of them had to leave,
and we --I've never did anything about it.
A: What if you fought back?
R: Well, we might have been able to fought back if we'd a stayed away
from there, if went yonder somewhere, but we had to got out of the
South. 
A: Didn't this make you and your family bitter?
R: Oh, sure, I use to be good to white folks like they are to me; I
just didn't like them, but I found out -- I studied, you know, I
studied the white folks, and I found out that I had some might good
friends among the white folks. Had it not been for my friends amongst
them, I'd a been dead, so I valued those friends very highly because
they was friends at the right place at the right time.
