I. It has been said that there
are certain five and ten dollar 
bills that will be of no value
after the war. This is a rumor.

II. A soldier was sitting in
a dentists chair at Camp Grant
and was asked to open his
mouth wide. "Gosh," exclaimed 
the soldier, "this is the first
chance I've had to open my
mouth since I came here."

III. A comedian over the radio
was describing countries
after the war. He then said, 
"Let's tune in on Tokyo
in 1946" there was a slight
pause and he continued,
"Well, well, what do ya know
no Tokyo."
