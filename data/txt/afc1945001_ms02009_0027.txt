Though the emphasis in the organization of rumor control projects
is on their responsibilities as information centers, rumor analysis
may point to an undesirable condition about which something can be done.
Where improving such conditions would eliminate rumors which tend to
interfere with the war effort, the project must consider improving such
conditions part of its business. Personel of the project should therefore include
[It should, therefore, have on its Advisory Board]
professional social service workers alert to the possibilities
for community action.

Owing to the complexity of the tasks confronting a rumor control 
project, it is important to have a balanced central Advisory Board.
This Board should include responsible community officials; security officers; 
social service workers; representatives of civic and service clubs, women's organizations,
labor and farm groups, consumer groups, and all minority groups in the
community; technical experts such as persons from the psychology or sociology 
department of local colleges or universities; newspaper and radio
representation.

With respect to informational programs on local subjects, the [local] project is in 
the best position to fill the community need. For blanketing the non-local 
rumors, the Office of War Information is prepared to cooperate and 
furnish material on the basis of analyses sent to the Office.
