lonesome, full-voiced, brooding notes pierced the stillness of a perfect night, indescribable and unforgettable. Starting on a low note the cry reached a crescendo in such pervasive volume and intensity that it seemed to fill the black void of darkness. The sound came from everywhere and nowhere. Then the cry shaded downward with the lower parts thrice repeated. Suddenly silence.
"That's Enoch crossing the long bridge from Livingston," said Mrs. Tartt. "He always stops at night in the middle of the bridge and gives that call. I have asked him to come to see you but he is very shy."
