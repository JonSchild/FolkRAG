When hard pressed by pursuing Texas Rangers, he would pay a farmer two hundred dollars for a horse the farmer valued at half that sum.  What wonder that the common people loved him and covered his tracks as he rode for years over Texas, robbing banks and holding up trains as his needs required.  In the eighties, there were no telephones or radios or even telegraph lines to help locate him.
