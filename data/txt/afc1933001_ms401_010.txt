"I always said my prayers in the Penitentiary." said Iron
Head, the expert crap-shooter and much feared coon-can player.
The next day in Austin Governor Allred asked him: "How 
did you get the name of Iron Head ?"  "I wuz cuttin' wood on
de Ramsey State Farm at Angleton at a place called de lifetime
cut.  A tree fell down dat I wasn't expectin'. Day wuz
live-oak trees.   Some limbs hit my haid an' it broke 'em off,
didn't knock me down and it didn't stop me from me from wukkin' on.
Dem boys name Iron Head."
I had picked Iron Head, singer of English ballads, Little
John Henry, The Grey Goose, Black Betty, Shorty George, and
other "sinful songs," to be chauffeur and companion on a 
hunt for folk songs among southern penitentiaries and remote
negro communities.
Iron Head's singing was to inspire other negro songsters
to strive to excel him.
