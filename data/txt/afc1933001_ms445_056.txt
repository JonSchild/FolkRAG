OUTLAWS

1. Pass Around the Bottle. A.A.F.S. 1601B.
Walter Williams, Salyersville, Ky. Sept. 1937.

2. Sweet Thing. A.A.F.S. 241B1. Nameless convict
on the Cummins State Farm, Arkansas. 1935.
See: Sharp, V. 2, p. 357,  . Version 2 comes
from Knott County, Ky. In the past ten years
popular over the radio and on the phonograph.

3. Three Nights Drunk. A.A.F.S. 1351B1. Mr. and Mrs.
E.C. Ball, Rugby, Va. Oct. 1937. See: Our Goodman,
Child, 274; Sharp, V.1, p. 267; Scarborough, p. 231ff.

4. Darling Corey. A.A.F.S.   . Aunt Molly Jackson,
New York City. See Victor Record #35838, banjo accompaniment.

5. Stavin' Chain. A.A.F.S. 210A. Tricky Sam, Huntsville,
Texas. Guitar accompaniment.

6. My Father Gave Me A Lump of Gold. A.A.F.S. 173A2.
Mrs. Emma Dusenberry, Mena, Arkansas. 1936.
See: Sharp, V.2, p. 79; Jackson, p. 68.

7. As I Sat Down to Play. A.A.F.S. #154313. Howard
Horne, Hazard, Ky. Oct. 1937. See: Scarborough, N.
p. 87; Gardner, 335, Victor record 20534B.
