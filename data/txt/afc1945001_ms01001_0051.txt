It will be immediately obvious that some rumors are completely local. "Mr. Jones, the President of X Plant, is doing thus and so with the funds of the Employees Welfare and Recreational System." Other rumors will represent local variations on a national theme. "So and so is not keeping to posted ceiling prices." Still other rumors will be clearly non-local, such as reference to state matters or personnel or federal matters and personnel. It is the function of the Bureau of Intelligence of the Office of War Information to organize the non-local material and all such rumors should be referred to them for treatment. These rumors will be analyzed by them and appropriate recommendations to the proper agencies will be made. As situations develop, the Bureau of Intelligence will provide special information describing the classification schemes which are to be used for non-local rumors, and the first processing of these rumors should be undertaken in the local projects. This will be done in order to provide improved perspective for those responsible for local rumor programs. It should be clear that non-local rumors must not be used in the local rumor campaigns except at the express direction of the Office of War Information. LOCAL RUMOR PROJECTS SHOULD DEAL WITH LOCAL RUMORS.

The Research Director will develop his classification schemes for the categorization of local rumors as the program develops. Inasmuch as it is the function of analysis to provide the basis for the educational programs it will be important for the Research Director to work closely with the Education Director in deciding what types of analysis would be most useful to the latter.
