Raine, supposed to be the composer. 
Mrs. Raine gave us tea & cakes & 
Raine told us his g-father was not
the man - the author, however, of 
other ballad-like poems - see 
pencilled pages later in this [p] book. 
He knew only the chorus, said that 
the fellow that composed it was sacked
by the company & so blacklisted
that he had to shift clean out 
of the country to find work.

Earlier we had called on 
Mark Anderson, 77, little,
rosy, spry, blue-eyed, spirited,
lead miner. Row of grey stone houses
against a hill, gardens in front,
