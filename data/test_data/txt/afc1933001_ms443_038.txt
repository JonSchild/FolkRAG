What's de matter wid Billy;
O Lawd, Lawd, Lawd;
What's de matter wid Billy;
O Lawd!

He [Billy] won' work, suh,
O Lawd, Lawd, Lawd;
He [Billy] won' work, suh,
O Lawd!

Po' Billy got fevo, *
O Lawd, Lawd, Lawd;
Billy got fevo,
O Lawd!

Hund'ed an' nine, suh,
O Lawd, Lawd, Lawd,
Hund'ed an' nine, suh,
O Lawd!

Billy in [de] [the] de lowlands
O Lawd, Lawd, Lawd;
Billy in de lowlands
O Lawd!

And on and on monton[or]ously, dispiritedly, repetitiously.

*"Fevo," fever, malaria, perhaps, or the condition leading to sunstroke, "where a man can't sweat.
Often a good captain in a good humor will
send a [sick] man [to] who gets sick in the field to the camp 
hospital. But, woe betide a man suspected of malingering! 
They'll whip his britches off.
