THE COMMUNITY RUMOR CONTROL PROJECT
News is ordinarily thought of as a report of day-to-day happenings.
Sometimes in the course of such a process, interest runs ahead of the 
facts available, and rumors fill in where information fails. Rumor study
will reveal where the need for more information lies, and the rumor control
project can then take the necessary steps to satisfy the needs of the 
community.

The rumor control project should see itself primarily as an
information agency in the community, taking on itself the responsibility 
for detecting and meeting the unfilled gaps in information. The project 
should get the corporation of local newspapers, radio, libraries, any
and all other established information centers. Materials to supply the 
information needed to cover community matters can be found locally. If,
in addition, the project wishes to concern itself with non-local rumors,
it can summarize and report them to the Office of War Information which
will try to supply necessary information material. Sometimes this will
already have been prepared but not widely enough distributed, or it might
happen that an analysis of rumors from your and other groups might show
the need for preparations of new facts for wider distribution.

The first responsibility of a rumor control project is, therefore, 
the collection and analysis of rumors. In order to have a truly
representative collection of rumors, representatives of all groups must
work with the rumor control project. This would include representatives 
of civic clubs, unions, minorities, educators, professional men. In a 
large community, rumor reporters are desirable; in a small community,
they are not. Reports should be submitted in writing, signed by the reporter.
