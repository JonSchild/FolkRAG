                        "I feel that I am better qualified to judge
                mess conditions than an officer who eats with us
                possibly twice a week. The food is probably
                alright before it falls into the hands of the
                cooks, but from that point on things start to
                happen. Most of the cooks could not qualify for
                a hamburg stand in the vicinity of the Barbary
                Coast (Phila.). Filth predominates. Brushes are
                used to scrub floors one moment and dishes the
                next. Pitchers are used for many purposes for
                which they were not originally intended. 

                        "I have seen dishes and cups cracked and
                nicked-- yet we still use them. Last night for a
                beverage-- a can of tomato juice was dumped into
                a barrel of water. 

                        "There have been times when the amount of food
                wasted would feed 50 people-- if they could eat it.
                Frankly I eat in the mess hall less than 50% of
                my meals." 
                                                Yours very truly,
