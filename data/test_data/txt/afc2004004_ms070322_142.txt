The Hebrides is hard old rock,
worn down now by wind & water
& time, nearly into the sea. Barely
a shim of green & brown moss for
a cap & a covering. Old earth's worn grinders
sticking up here through the cold
grey blue porridge bowl of the
Atlantic. Arctic in feeling. Hard
dark shadows against a clear
sky. Hard as whisky is harsh.
Granite boulders with shadows of
olive, purple & brown. Nothing
much left, but still a shelter for
the liveliest Celtic culture left
anywhere.
