Port Aransas, Texas,
2/7/1939.

Dear Mr. Spivacke: I am asking
Mr. Greene to wire you for any
concessions about the Negro folk song
material. It is a matter of relief
to me that the records are by now
back in your hands. Hereafter, for
any reason, you may feel sure
that I shall not again make
a similar request of you. Wont
you please send [Cle] Mr.
Greene's letter up for Mr. Putman's
reading? He heard from your
predecessor in office so many
aspersions on my persistence
[*over*]
