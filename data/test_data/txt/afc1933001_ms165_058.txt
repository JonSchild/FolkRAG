UNIVERSITY OF KENTUCKY
Lexington
Radio Programs first half of 1942
All times are Central Standard.
Symbols are as follows:-
WHAS - WHAS, Louisville
WLA - WLAP, Lexington
MBS - the network of the Mutual Broadcasting System
SN - Southern Network
In addition to the broadcasts listed, many specials will occur
during this period.

Mondays
12:50 to 1:00 p.m. Agricultural Program. WHAS. ("Engineering
on the Farm," by Earl G. Welch, field agent
in Agricultural Engineering, first Monday of each month.)
1:30 to 1:45 p.m. "Keyboard Appreciation," by Ross Todd,
pianist, through May 25, SN.
1:45 to 2:00 p.m. "Kentucky's Mineral Wealth," by Daniel J.
Jones, Kentucky State Geologist, through March 9. "Ancient 
Minds in a Modern World," by Dr. Irwin T. Sanders,
associate professor of Sociology, through May 25. SN.

Tuesdays
12:50 to 1:00 p.m. Agricultural Program. WHAS. ("Farm
Management Forum," by L. A. Bradford, assistant professor
of Farm Management, first Tuesday of each month.)
1:30 to 1:45 p.m. "The Voice of the Free," through March 31.
"Your Speech and What it Tells," by Dr. L. L. Dantzler,
professor of Philology, through May 26. MBS.
1:45 to 2:00 p.m. "Geography of the Pacific," by David M.
M. Young, assistant professor of Geology. MBS.
4:45 to 5:00 p.m. University High School, through May 26.
WLAP.

Wednesdays
12:50 to 1:00 p.m. "Doings of Kentucky Farm Folk," by C.
A. Lewis, editor, Agricultural Extension Division. WHAS.
1:30 to 1:45 pm. "The Nation's Business Week by Week," by
Dr. E. Z. Palmer, professor of Economics, through May
27. SN.
1:45 to 2:00 p.m. "Book Theatre," through May 27. MBS.

Thursdays
12:50 to 1:00 p.m. Agricultural Program. WHAS. ("Agricultural
Market Review," by Dana Card, associate professor
of Agricultural Economics, first Thursday of each
month.)
1:30 to 1:45 p.m. "American Song Bag," through May 28.
MBS.
1:45 to 2:00 p.m. "Stories from the Western Hemisphere,"
through May 28. MBS.

Fridays
12:50 to 1:00 p.m. "What Farm Folk are Asking," by L. C.
Brewer, College of Agriculture. WHAS.

Saturdays
12:00 to 12:30 p.m. "The Wildcat Review," through May 30.
"Summer Fun Under the Summer Sun," through summer.
WHAS.
12:50 to 1:00 p.m. "Here and There with the Homemakers."
WHAS.
2:00 to 2:30 p.m. starting June 6 (time tentative). "Folklore
of the Southern Appalachians." MBS.

Sundays
12:00 to 12:30 p.m. "Home Fires." WHAS.
