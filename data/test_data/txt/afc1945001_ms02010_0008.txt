The analysis of rumors can best be done by persons trained in the social
sciences. Local universities will generally have experts on their staffs who can
be asked to serve on the project and supervise the analysis.

Rumor analysis should indicate what rumors are being spread, how widely, and
in what circles. Such analyses reveal what the rumor problems are, how serious
they are, and toward whom information must be directed.

Control Programs--Information

Once it has been decided to blanket a given field of rumors with information,
[every information] all outlets available to the project should be used. [The publicity outlets used] These 
should include newspapers, group and club publications, lectures
before civic and club groups, radio interviews, speeches, or programs, pamphlets,
school projects, library displays, discussion group materials, etc. The project
might consider the value of asking editors to contribute space for columns called
"Did You Know That?" or "Things You Ought to Know," the project providing colorful and dramatic information designed to fight rumors without specifically mentioning them.

The Continuing Program

In addition to the special educational campaigns worked out on the basis of
informational gaps, the continuing information purpose of a rumor control project
is to toughen people's minds against rumors and the repetition of rumors.
