He paid him $300 for his claim, and we had plenty meat [from them]. I remember the first time I just had all the good ham meat and biscuits I wanted (laughter). One day my father sent me down in the [mine] bottom to split rail timbers, you know to fix the fence. And my sister just cut a whole lot of [that good old] ham and cooked a lot of biscuits and gave me a great big bucketful to carry along with me. There was a cool spring of water down in the bottom and I ate more ham and biscuits than I split rails. (laughter). That was one of the happiest days of [our] my life.
[**A**] How old about were you then Rev?
[**R**] I don't remember exactly but I [guess] suppose I was about fifteen years old, yes, sir, about fifteen years old.
[**A**] And there had been times before, I guess, when you had been hungry -- plenty.
Oh, yes, there had been times when we didn't have anything to eat but beans and cornbread [and bacon]
[and Drinking?]
Not too much of that. And lots of times I was hungry, but we just had to work.
[**A**] What did your Mom and Poppa say at times like that? Weren't they sad and --?
[**R**] Oh, yes, they [said] would say to us children, "work hard and it will be better after a while."
[It must have been pretty] They had seen really hard times under slavery, I guess.
