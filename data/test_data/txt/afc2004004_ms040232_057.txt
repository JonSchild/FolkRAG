Macintosh's Lament
The traditional account of the origin of this
pibrch is as follows:
It was predicted that Macintosh
would die by the agency of
a fine black steed that he had,
out of bravado he continued to ride this
horse.
On the day of his marriage he
was riding the black horse, but as it
grew restive and would not be quieted,
he shot it dead.
After the marriage the bride and
her entourage came riding away from
the church on palfreys, and Macintosh
followed on a roan.  When the roan
passed the corpse of the black horse
it shied at it, and Macintosh was
thrown and killed.
