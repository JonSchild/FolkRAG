                                 Match Box Blues                             (16)                                                                                                                      

P.g. has got my babe an' gone,
Oh, de P.G. has got my gal an' gone,
P.G. done got my gal an' gone.

Lef' me here jus' to sing thins song, (2)
Oh de P.G. has got my gal an' gone.

Now this here's a song that I compose about de P.G.----
it done been here an' got her an' gone-----an' this song was an
old man/he had married a woman thought/an' she had got ev'y dollar that
he had------but she was a little mistaken--- but anyhow she had
got so much of it that it was worryin' him ---- he was settin'
wid his head hung down an' she pass by him an' ask him, say,
"Daddy, what's the matter wid you?"-----He looked up at her an'
commence a-cryin'----

"Settin' down here wonderin' would a match -box hol' my clo's.(3)"

She says, "Daddy, what in the worl'd the use for you cryin' like
that fer?--- What in the world you got on your min'?------
he sit down an' 'gin to worry agin------
"I don' want to be bothered with no suitcase on my road." (3)

She walked up to him put her arm aroun' him---- He looked
up at her an' said, "I'm goin' down to see my frien' -- an' see
what would he do if his baby's packin' up her trunk?"-----
he walked down to his frien's an' here what he asked his frien'-----
Sung; "What would you do when your gal's packin' up her trunk?" (3)

The frien' run out an' tole him the bes' egvice he could------
"Get you a half a gallon o' whiskey an' git on you a big drunk." (3)
