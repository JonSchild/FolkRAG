My father was a weaver poor
As ever filled a spool, sir;
There never was beef within the door
But half a pun at yule, sir

When I was six I went to school
Because it was the fashion;
And ilka Sunday to the kirk, 
To save me fae a thrashin?

They learned me to read and write
And count the rule o' three, sir,
When a noble thought cam' into my heid
That a soldier I would be sir

As I went down to Dunniston
A place in Futon county,
I liked there sir Sergeant Brown
For forty pounds o' [???]
