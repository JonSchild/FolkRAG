Fisk, 9. 14.

Mr. Chapman is a chief
Brave xx a man as ever lived
Never r[a]un and never will.
His main occupation is
Taking copper still[s].

Mr. Rivers take a quart;
Mr. Ferd took a pint;
Mr. Chapman has to have
The whole damn jinte.

He'll [run] rest and put you
in [Kahoma] Co'homa County Jail;
You can get Bud Doggett
To go your bail.
[Hear that cop’ll] Judge Allen Cou’ld fine you
For possession and sale;
[In] Give gon six long months
In [Kahoma] Co’homa County Jail.

Now you may come here
Big and stout,
But these [flat] cold iron b[a]unks
will wear your black ass out.

A: Who made that up?
S: A boy in jail, named Howard Span.
A: He was put in there for this offense?
S: Yes.
A: Do you know any more toasts, Will?
S: I don't think I do.
A: Maybe we'd like em even if you don't.
S: Somehow I can't think of um like I ought to. I know [loads] a lots of um.
Preacher and Deacon's Wife, Asa Wave
Preacher stayed on one night in the deacon's house, you know, [A]and this 
deacon had a good-lookin wife[. A] , and this preacher liked her. So the 
deacon told the preacher, said, "Brother, now you can [around] lay down there 
until you git ready to go up. I'm goin up here to plow some." He 
told him, "All right."
So he was laying in the bed, and his wife just kept passing back and 
forth through the room. And the preacher ahd done got up, you know.
