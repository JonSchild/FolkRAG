JOHN A. LOMAS
7456 SAN BENITO WAY
DALLAS, TEXAS
4/21/1941

Dear Alan:

1. Please read the enclosed letter.
2. Autograph the copy of the Lead
Belly book which Mrs. Sharp is sending
you today and forward it at once
to Geo. A. Hill III. I enclose postage.
3. George A. Hill, III, the oldest son
of a University friend, since September
1940 has been a victim of Infantile Paralysis.
He is a 19 year old Princeton
Junior, the champion boxer in his weight
for that university. Now he is completely
paralyzed from the waist down. His
father tells me that our Folk Songs
have helped to save the boy from
utter despair. He has learned to play
them on his guitar, is a devotee
of Lead Belly in particular, owns all
the Lead Belly records, fiddles with
