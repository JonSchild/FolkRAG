A HISTORY AND PHILOSOPHY OF THE TOPANGA CONTEST
 
The contest was begun in 1961 by Peg Benepe
 
and Margot Slocum, housewives, to raise money (in
 
a fun way) for their church building fund.  Also
 
they wanted to encourage people to enjoy and play
 
the banjo and fiddle music that they had learned
 
to love.
 
The idea got a very enthusiastic response
 
from the folk music community, with donations of
 
time by people like Bess Hawes, Sam Hinton and
 
Frank Hamilton.
 
500 people attended the first contest at Ian
 
Thiermann's home in a wooded canyon in Topanga.
 
There were 26 banjo players and 5 fiddlers.  '
The
 
winners were Don Parmalee, Stu Jamieson and Richard
 
Greene.
 
Year after year, everyone important in folk
 
music came and gave of their time and effort; the
 
staff at McCabe's, the people at the Ash Grove and
 
at UCLA.
 The personnel changed, the location
 
changed but the contest went on.
 
Each year we tried to bring together diverse
 
groups of folk musicians,  (man  of whom often had
 
no use for each other's music ! and make the con-
test a yearly meeting ground where all styles
 
could be heard and appreciated.  We wanted to keep
 
the atmosphere casual so that a beginner could
 
step up and play his best or an advanced player
 
could relax and create, and not just play a "set
 
piece."
 
Although no longer in a rustic shady setting,
 
we hope the contest still contains the qualities
 
it began with:
 1. An appreciation of beginner
 
and intermediate players. 2
. Small prizes donated
 
by interested backers (nobody can get yery mad if
 
they don't win.
 It's all in fun).3. The donation
 
of all proceeds to charities (presently The Ameri-
can Friend's Service Committee and The John Edwards
 
Memorial- Folk Archive at UCLA). 4
. Volunteers to
 
put it on.
 The Manager, Dorian Keyser (pronounced
 
Kaiser); The Secretary-Treasurer, Dalia Keyser;
 
The Prize
,/Music Chairman, Mary Ellen Clark; and The
 
Gate Chairman, Ian Thiermann; all give their time,
 
as do the judges, ticket takers, ushers and sound
 
A men.  Even the sound system is provided at no
 
charge by The Guitar Shop in Pasadena.
 
It would be nice to have it in Topanga again
 
when the County Supervisors relax their restric-
tions on outdoor music festivals, but until that
 
happy time, we'll continue to call it the Topanga
 
Banjo Fiddle Contest, and enjoy it as it is.
