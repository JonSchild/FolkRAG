Accession	  Number:	  AU2015-­‐1003	  
Interviewee:	  Bill	  Suhr	  and	  Andrea	  Scott	  
Interviewer:	  Andy	  Kolovos	  
Interview	  Date:	  March	  6,	  2015	  
Interview	  Location:	  Champlain	  Orchards,	  Shoreham,	  VT	  
	  
This	  is	  the	  first	  interview	  in	  a	  series	  aimed	  at	  exploring	  the	  network	  of	  growers	  and	  
producers	  who	  supply	  Fletcher	  Allen	  Hosptial/University	  of	  Vermont	  Medical	  
Center.	  
	  
Greg	  and	  I	  were	  both	  angling	  to	  interview	  Bill	  Suhr—unbeknownst	  to	  one	  another.	  	  
This	  resulted	  in	  some	  confusion	  with	  Bill	  and	  his	  wife	  Andrea	  since	  he	  and	  I	  had	  
both	  contacted	  them	  independently	  and	  ended	  up	  scheduling	  two	  separate	  
interviews.	  	  We	  did	  eventually	  straighten	  it	  all	  out	  (“Oh!	  That	  was	  why	  she	  thought	  I	  
had	  called	  already!”)	  and	  Greg	  and	  I	  settled	  that	  he	  would	  do	  the	  interview	  with	  
them	  and	  bring	  along	  Susannah	  White,	  our	  intern.	  Ultimately,	  however,	  that’s	  not	  
how	  it	  worked	  out	  and	  Susannah	  and	  I	  ended	  up	  interviewing	  Bill	  and	  Andrea	  on	  the	  
date	  Greg	  had	  scheduled	  to	  meet	  with	  them.	  
	  
The	  orchard	  complex	  is	  deep	  in	  the	  heart	  of	  agricultural	  Addison	  County	  and	  it	  is	  
large.	  	  I	  had	  some	  difficulty	  figuring	  out	  where	  to	  meet	  Bill	  once	  I	  arrived	  on	  site.	  	  
Susannah	  and	  I	  found	  each	  other	  and	  decided	  to	  go	  into	  their	  retail	  store	  to	  see	  if	  we	  
could	  figure	  out	  how	  to	  find	  Bill.	  This	  proved	  to	  be	  the	  best	  choice	  as	  the	  folks	  at	  the	  
store	  called	  up	  to	  the	  office	  and	  Bill	  eventually	  came	  down	  to	  meet	  us.	  	  He	  wanted	  us	  
to	  follow	  him	  to	  his	  house	  so	  his	  wife	  could	  be	  present	  for	  the	  interview	  as	  well,	  and	  
Susannah	  and	  I	  piled	  into	  my	  car	  and	  followed	  him	  home	  around	  the	  corner.	  
	  
Bill	  and	  Andrea	  were	  able	  to	  give	  us	  about	  an	  hour	  of	  their	  time	  and	  we	  had	  what	  
amounted	  to	  a	  fairly	  quick	  discussion	  of	  the	  history	  of	  the	  operation,	  its	  viability	  and	  
their	  approach	  to	  raising	  fruit.	  We	  were	  interrupted	  part	  way	  though	  by	  a	  neighbor	  
stopping	  by	  to	  chat—one	  who	  had	  recently	  sold	  off	  her	  family’s	  operation	  (beef,	  I	  
believe?),	  and	  we	  had	  an	  interesting	  and	  relevant	  discussion	  about	  her	  experiences	  
as	  well.	  
	  
This	  interview	  and	  its	  pace	  in	  many	  ways	  set	  the	  tone	  for	  the	  interviews	  to	  follow.	  
Working	  farmers	  don’t	  have	  a	  lot	  of	  time	  to	  spare	  and	  with	  many	  of	  the	  later	  
interviews	  a	  majority	  of	  the	  interviewees	  were	  really	  only	  able	  to	  commit	  about	  an	  
hour	  of	  their	  time	  to	  talk.	  	  The	  result	  is	  kind	  of	  a	  more	  bare	  bones	  interview	  that’s	  
focused	  on	  some	  key	  questions.	  	  This	  is	  a	  bit	  of	  a	  bummer,	  but	  apparently	  the	  way	  
it’s	  simply	  got	  to	  be	  for	  many	  of	  the	  interviewees.
