Dear Bess,
 
Seeing your wonderful  picture in the Times
 
on Sunday brought back many dear memories of the halcyon
 
days of hootenannies,  folksongs, protests and sixties
 
nostalgia.
 I am now in my seventies,  enjoying every
 
minute,  still singing those everlasting songs you taught.
 
So many friends asked me to "teach"  them.
 All I did was
 
pass on,  to the best of my ability, what you so carefully
 
and lovingly gave to your classes.
 This is a thankyou for
 
the songs which will never die,  the folklore,  the fun and
 
just plain music you gave to us all.
 None of us "locals"
 
will ever forget it. Or you.
 My children and grandchildren
 
play and love the songs and hand-games from here and round
 
eri e  world.
 A true legacy,  that's what it is.
 
Bess dear, we thank you from the
 
bottom of our hearts.

June  17,  1995
 
I write belatedly to express my pride and pleasure in having
 
been awarded the title of Emeritus Professor.  During my
 
teaching years,  although I enjoyed the hospitality of a
 
number of west  coast  campuses,  CSUN was where I truly learned
 
my trade,  so to say;  and it  still feels exactly like home,
 
whenever  I get a chance to visit.  The  status of an Emeritus
 
will make my trips to campus even more comfortable and happy.
 
Actually,  I am hopeful that  in another year or so I will be
 
able to take more frequent advantage of the privileges you
 
have  just granted me.  The ties that have held me in the
 
Washington D.C.  area are loosening these days,  and I am
 
thinking seriously of relocating permanently on the west
 
coast.  Perhaps  in such a case  I can find some small way to
 
contribute a bit more to the good and welfare of the
 
University.
 
In any event,  during my next visit to the area,  I will hope
 
to have an opportunity to drop by your office and express  in
 
person my appreciation for this honor.  I am only sorry I
 
could not attend the Honored Faculty Reception of May 18;
 
distance combined with family illnesses made  it  impossible.
 
I thank you and my old friends and colleagues  in the School
 
of Social and Behavioral Sciences once again.
 
Sincerely,
 
Bess Lomax Hawes
