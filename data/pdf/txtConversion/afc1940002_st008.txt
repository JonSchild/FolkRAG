The first strophe of this ballad contains an exhortation to the faithful to come join the Virgin
Mary in her sorrows. In the remaining part of the hymn, the Passion of Our Lord is passed
in review, and much of this lyric is in dialogue form, with the Virgin Mary doing most of the
speaking. Part of the time she summons mothers in general to share her sorrows and part
of the time she addresses her own Son.
