The faithful are entreated to join the Virgin in her sufferings during the Passion. Part of the
time, Mary addresses her Son and gives vent to her grief. The last two strophes contain
expressions of farewell addressed by the faithful to the Virgin.
This hymn is sung immediately after the recitation of the rosary at nightfall on Good Friday.

This  alabado  is sung after the seizure of Our Lord by the Roman soldiers, during the
procession on Good Friday. It covers most of the events of the Passion.
