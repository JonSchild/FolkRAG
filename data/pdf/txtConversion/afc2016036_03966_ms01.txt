Abstract: 
Pablo Sam immigrated to New Bedford, MA from Guatemala in 2004 and works at Bergie’s 
Seafood Inc. He started as a fish cutter and over the last couple of years has become a machine 
operator and a mechanic for 50 percent of the machines at his location. Pablo enjoys his work 
and plans on becoming a more efficient mechanic for the company. 
Interview with Pablo Sam, May 7, 2017 
[2:15] Life in Guatemala, poor economy in Guatemala, jobs are difficult and temporary, decision 
to migrate to the U.S. 
[5:23] First job was many years ago at Bob’s Tire, next job at Bergie’s seafood (8+ years). 
Machine operator filleting fish, gaining mechanical skills for all fillet machinery, trained by a 
senior mechanic at Bergie’s dedicated work for personal life goals 
[10:11] The process of cutting and filleting fish by hand, fast paced hand cutting- 300 fish an 
hour, the process of fillet and scaling fish machines, importance of precision when filleting fish 
[15:13] New machinery is able to debone and fillet fishes, job enjoyment, problem solving skills 
are essential for mechanic work 
[19:40] Weekly oil change and checkup, tune up for all machines before the week begins every 
Sunday, weekly checks are crucial to maintain a working machine. Shifts begin at 7:00 AM and 
end when productivity dies down, normally in the evening at five. Productivity goes up and 
down depending on the boats and time of year. Summer time is ok. Experience improves career. 
[24:37] Employees are a great mixture of many different ethnic groups, most workers speak 
Spanish, family, struggles of the economy in Guatemala. 
[29:57] Personal sacrifices for family, safety is important to avoid injuries. Machine operation 
can cause serious injury if not careful. Machines handles large 15-20 lbs. knives, company is 
growing and increasing productivity and improving factory technology, hours have increased 
over the years from 20-30 hours a week eight years ago to 50-60 hours a week now. 
[37:06] Machine mechanic work is time consuming, learning mechanic work is more than 
reading, mechanic work is situational and about problem solving. 
[42:07] End of Interview
